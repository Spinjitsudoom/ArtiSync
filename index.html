Everything you need to clean up your library

🎵

Spotify Metadata

Search any artist, browse every release with album art thumbnails, and pull accurate track names, years, and genres directly from Spotify's database.

🔍

Fuzzy File Matching

Automatically maps your local audio files to the correct tracks using edit-distance scoring — works even with messy or incomplete filenames.

🏷️

Tag Writing

Embeds artist, album, title, year, genre, track number, disc number, and cover art into ID3v2 (MP3), Vorbis (FLAC/OGG), and MP4/M4A atoms.

🖼️

Cover Art

Downloads the highest-resolution image from Spotify's CDN and embeds it into your files. The sidebar shows 64×64 thumbnails for every release as they load.

🔄

Audio Conversion

Convert between MP3, FLAC, M4A, OGG, and WAV via FFmpeg. AAC↔M4A is remuxed losslessly; all other pairs are transcoded with Low / Medium / High / Best quality presets.

↩️

Preview & Undo

See exactly which files will be renamed and what their new names will be before committing. Every execute operation can be fully reversed.

📁

Batch Mode

Process an entire artist directory in one click — the app auto-detects and matches every album sub-folder without manual selection.

🎨

9 Colour Themes

Dark, Light, Midnight, Emerald, Amethyst, Crimson, Forest, Ocean, and Slate — switch instantly from File → Settings.

How it works

1

Browse to your music folder

Point the app at a root directory containing artist sub-folders. The path is remembered between sessions.

2

Artist is auto-detected

The app searches Spotify for the folder name and populates the releases sidebar with album art thumbnails loading in the background.

3

Select a release

Click any album or single. The app fetches the full track list, cover art, and metadata from Spotify.

4

Review the match preview

The Preview tab shows every file → new name mapping with a confidence percentage. High-confidence matches are green; uncertain ones are amber.

5

Execute Rename + Tag

One click renames all files, writes embedded tags (title, artist, album, year, genre, track number), and embeds the cover art.

6

Undo if needed

Changed your mind? Hit Undo to restore the original filenames instantly.
